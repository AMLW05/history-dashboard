Question 1

Question Text
According to the immigration data, what year saw PEAK immigration to the United States before World War I?
Answer Options (Enter all 4 in Canvas)
A 1907, with 1,285,000 immigrants CORRECT
B 1890, with 455,000 immigrants
C 1915, with 430,000 immigrants
D 1900, with 800,000 immigrants
Feedback (Enter in Canvas feedback fields)
Correct Answer Feedback
Correct! 1907 marked peak immigration at 1,285,000 - nearly triple the 1890 level. The Panic of 1907 and later WWI (1914) caused sharp declines.
Incorrect Answer Feedback
Look at the immigration data line chart. Find the highest point on the graph and its corresponding year.
Question 2
Canvas Type: Multiple Answer
2.5 points
Question Text
Based on the immigration data, immigration declined sharply after 1907. Select ALL factors that contributed to this decline. (Select all that apply)
Answer Options (Mark multiple correct in Canvas)
A The Panic of 1907 (economic recession) reduced job opportunities CORRECT
B Growing restrictions on immigration through legislation CORRECT
C World War I disrupted Atlantic travel beginning in 1914 CORRECT
D Immigration was completely banned by federal law in 1908
Feedback
Correct Answer Feedback
Correct! Multiple factors converged: economic recession (1907), increasing restrictions (literacy tests, head taxes), and WWI (1914) disrupting ocean travel all contributed to declining immigration.
Incorrect Answer Feedback
Immigration was not completely banned in 1908. Consider economic, legislative, and international factors that might have reduced immigration after 1907.

Categories to Create in Canvas
Category 1: Why Western States Supported Suffrage
Category 2: Why Eastern/Southern States Resisted
Items to Categorize (with correct category)
Why Western States Supported Suffrage (Category 1)
Frontier societies had fewer established gender hierarchies
Suffrage seen as way to attract female settlers and promote "civilization"
Smaller populations made it easier to experiment with reforms
Why Eastern/Southern States Resisted (Category 2)
Entrenched political machines opposed new voters they couldn't control
Fear that women's suffrage would lead to Black women voting in the South

General Feedback
Regional differences in suffrage reflected broader patterns: Western progressivism and experimentation vs. Eastern machine politics and Southern racial anxieties about expanding the electorate.

Question 6
Canvas Type: Multiple Choice
2.5 points
Question Text
The labor strike data shows strikes more than doubled from 1,900 (1890) to 4,450 (1904). What does this pattern suggest about worker conditions during industrialization?
Answer Options (Enter all 4 in Canvas)
A Workers were increasingly dissatisfied with wages, hours, and conditions, leading to more organized resistance CORRECT
B Workers were completely satisfied but struck to support their employers
C The government encouraged strikes as official policy
D Strike numbers declined as conditions improved
Feedback
Correct Answer Feedback
Correct! Rising strikes reflected worker discontent with industrial conditions: long hours (10-12 hour days), dangerous workplaces, low wages, and lack of job security. Unions like the AFL organized this resistance.
Incorrect Answer Feedback
More strikes generally indicate more worker grievances, not satisfaction. Consider what conditions might motivate workers to strike.

Question 10
Canvas Type: Multiple Choice
2.5 points
Question Text
Considering all the data together, what does the evidence reveal about the LIMITATIONS of Progressive Era reforms?
Answer Options (Enter all 4 in Canvas)
A Reforms were often incomplete, regionally uneven, and slow to address root causes of social problems CORRECT
B Progressive reforms solved all social problems by 1920
C The data shows no reforms were attempted during this period
D Southern states led the nation in implementing Progressive reforms
Feedback
Correct Answer Feedback
Correct! The data shows reforms' limits: suffrage took 30 years and excluded many women of color; 1.75 million children still worked; strikes increased as underlying conditions persisted. Progress was real but uneven and incomplete.
Incorrect Answer Feedback
Consider what the data reveals about the pace and completeness of reform. Did all regions embrace reform equally? Were problems solved quickly or did they persist?
