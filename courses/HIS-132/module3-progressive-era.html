Quiz Questions (10 Questions, 25 Points Total)

Q1 Multiple Choice (2.5 pts)
According to the immigration data, what year saw PEAK immigration to the United States before World War I?
A 1907, with 1,285,000 immigrants
B 1890, with 455,000 immigrants
C 1915, with 430,000 immigrants
D 1900, with 800,000 immigrants
1907 marked peak immigration at 1,285,000 - nearly triple the 1890 level. The Panic of 1907 and later WWI (1914) caused sharp declines.
Q2 Multiple Answer (2.5 pts)
Based on the immigration data, immigration declined sharply after 1907. Select ALL factors that contributed to this decline. (Select all that apply)
A The Panic of 1907 (economic recession) reduced job opportunities
B Growing restrictions on immigration through legislation
C World War I disrupted Atlantic travel beginning in 1914
D Immigration was completely banned by federal law in 1908
Multiple factors converged: economic recession (1907), increasing restrictions, and WWI disrupting ocean travel.
Q3 Ordering (2.5 pts)
Based on the suffrage timeline, place these states/events in chronological order by when women gained voting rights (earliest to latest).
  1. Wyoming grants women's suffrage (1890)
  2. Colorado grants women's suffrage (1893)
  3. Utah and Idaho grant women's suffrage (1896)
  4. Washington and California grant women's suffrage (1910-1911)
  5. 19th Amendment ratified - nationwide suffrage (1920)
Western states led the suffrage movement. Eastern and especially Southern states resisted until forced by the 19th Amendment.
Q4 Categorization (2.5 pts)
The suffrage data shows Western states granted women's suffrage decades before Eastern and Southern states. Categorize each factor.
Why Western States Supported Suffrage
Frontier societies had fewer established gender hierarchies
Suffrage seen as way to attract female settlers and promote "civilization"
Smaller populations made it easier to experiment with reforms
Why Eastern/Southern States Resisted
Entrenched political machines opposed new voters they couldn't control
Fear that women's suffrage would lead to Black women voting in the South
Traditional gender roles more deeply embedded in established society
Q5 Numeric (2.5 pts)
According to the labor strike data, how many MORE strikes occurred in 1904 compared to 1890?
Answer: 2,550 (4,450 - 1,900 = 2,550)
This represents a 134% increase, demonstrating growing worker militancy during the Progressive Era.
Q6 Multiple Choice (2.5 pts)
The labor strike data shows strikes more than doubled from 1,900 (1890) to 4,450 (1904). What does this pattern suggest about worker conditions during industrialization?
A Workers were increasingly dissatisfied with wages, hours, and conditions, leading to more organized resistance
B Workers were completely satisfied but struck to support their employers
C The government encouraged strikes as official policy
D Strike numbers declined as conditions improved
Rising strikes reflected worker discontent with industrial conditions: long hours, dangerous workplaces, low wages, and lack of job security.

Immigration restriction legislation1.28 million immigrants in peak year 1907

Q8 Multiple Answer (2.5 pts)
The child labor data shows 1.75 million children working in 1900. Select ALL conclusions historians can draw from this statistic. (Select all that apply)
A Despite reform efforts, child labor remained widespread at the turn of the century
B Industrial capitalism relied heavily on cheap child labor
C Progressive child labor laws had limited success in the early period
D Child labor was completely eliminated by 1900
The 1.75 million figure shows child labor's persistence. Effective federal child labor regulation didn't come until the 1930s.
Q9 Categorization (2.5 pts)

Supported by the Data
Immigration and strike numbers both increased during the same period (1890-1904)
Immigrant workers often participated in labor strikes
Rising immigration coincided with industrial expansion and worker unrest
Both trends reflect the rapid industrialization transforming American society
Not Supported by the Data
Immigration caused strikes to decrease
Native-born workers never participated in strikes
Q10 Multiple Choice (2.5 pts)
Considering all the data together, what does the evidence reveal about the LIMITATIONS of Progressive Era reforms?
A Reforms were often incomplete, regionally uneven, and slow to address root causes of social problems
B Progressive reforms solved all social problems by 1920
C The data shows no reforms were attempted during this period
D Southern states led the nation in implementing Progressive reforms
The data shows reforms' limits: suffrage took 30 years; 1.75 million children still worked; strikes increased as underlying conditions persisted. Progress was real but uneven and incomplete.
