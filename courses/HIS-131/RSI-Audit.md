## REGULATORY BACKGROUND

### Federal Definition: "Regular and Substantive Interaction"

Per 34 CFR Â§ 600.2 (updated March 2020, clarified October 2022):

**"Regular interaction"** means:
- Predictable and scheduled throughout the term
- Initiated by instructor (not just responsive to student requests)
- Occurs throughout the entire course duration (not just at beginning or end)
- Sufficient frequency to support student learning

**"Substantive interaction"** means:
- Engagement with students on course content and learning
- Monitoring student progress and comprehension
- Providing feedback to improve student learning
- Does NOT include only administrative tasks (reminders, logistics, etc.)
- Must involve instructor expertise in the discipline

**Between instructor and students** means:
- Actual faculty member (not just TA or automated system)
- Two-way communication (not just broadcast announcements without engagement)
- Individualized or group-based interaction demonstrating instructor presence
