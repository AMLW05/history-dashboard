Analysis Questions

Q1: Why did Jefferson and Burr receive the same number of electoral votes?
Answer: Constitution didn't distinguish between President and VP; electors voted for two people
Q2: Which region gave strongest support to Federalists?
Answer: New England
Q3: Which region gave strongest support to Democratic-Republicans?
Answer: South and Western states (KY, TN)
Q4: What constitutional problem did this election expose?
Answer: Need to separate President and VP voting (led to 12th Amendment in 1804)
Q5: How was the tie between Jefferson and Burr resolved?
Answer: House of Representatives chose Jefferson after 36 ballots; Hamilton's influence helped
Q6: Why is this election called the 'Revolution of 1800'?
Answer: First peaceful transfer of power between opposing political parties in U.S. history
Q7: What does the regional pattern reveal about political divisions?
Answer: North-South divide with commercial North supporting Federalists, agrarian South supporting Republicans
