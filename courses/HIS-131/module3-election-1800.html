Question 1
Canvas Type: Multiple Choice
2.5 points
Question Text
According to the electoral vote data, why did Jefferson and Burr receive the same number of electoral votes (73 each)?
Answer Options (Enter all 4 in Canvas)
A The original Constitution did not distinguish between presidential and vice-presidential votes; each elector cast two votes for president CORRECT
B There was a tie in the popular vote between the two candidates
C The Federalists deliberately created a tie to prevent Jefferson from winning
D Each state was required to split its electoral votes equally between party nominees
Feedback (Enter in Canvas feedback fields)
Correct Answer Feedback
Correct! Under the original Constitution, electors voted for two people without distinguishing president from vice president. The person with the most votes became president, second-most became VP. This flaw led to the 12th Amendment (1804).
Incorrect Answer Feedback
Review the original electoral system under the Constitution. Before the 12th Amendment, electors voted for two people without specifying which office each vote was for.

Question Text
The Election of 1800 is often called the "Revolution of 1800." Based on the electoral data showing a Federalist defeat, which statement BEST explains why historians use this term?
Answer Options (Enter all 4 in Canvas)
A It marked the first peaceful transfer of power between opposing political parties in American history CORRECT
B It resulted in violent protests that nearly overthrew the government
C It was the first election where women were allowed to vote
D It eliminated the Electoral College system
Feedback
Correct Answer Feedback
Correct! Jefferson called it a "revolution" because power transferred peacefully from Federalists to Democratic-Republicans - proving the Constitution could handle political change without violence.
Incorrect Answer Feedback
The "revolution" was not violent but political. Consider what was unprecedented about the Federalists accepting defeat and yielding power to their opponents.
