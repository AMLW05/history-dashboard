Q1 Multiple Choice (2.5 pts)
According to the electoral vote data, why did Jefferson and Burr receive the same number of electoral votes (73 each)?
A The original Constitution did not distinguish between presidential and vice-presidential votes; each elector cast two votes for president
B There was a tie in the popular vote between the two candidates
C The Federalists deliberately created a tie to prevent Jefferson from winning
D Each state was required to split its electoral votes equally between party nominees
Under the original Constitution, electors voted for two people without distinguishing president from vice president. This flaw led to the 12th Amendment (1804).

Q4 Ordering (2.5 pts)
Place the following events of the Election of 1800 crisis in chronological order, from first to last.
  1. Electoral votes counted, revealing Jefferson-Burr tie at 73 votes each
  2. Election moved to House of Representatives as required by Constitution
  3. House deadlocked through 35 ballots over multiple days
  4. Hamilton's influence helps break deadlock in favor of Jefferson

Hamilton, despite opposing Jefferson's politics, believed Burr was more dangerous and privately urged Federalists to support Jefferson.
Q5 Numeric (2.5 pts)
Based on the electoral vote data, calculate the total number of electoral votes cast in the Election of 1800.
Answer: 276 (73 + 73 + 65 + 64 + 1 = 276)
Each elector cast 2 votes, and there were 138 electors, for a total of 276 electoral votes.

Q7 Multiple Choice (2.5 pts)
The Election of 1800 is often called the "Revolution of 1800." Based on the electoral data showing a Federalist defeat, which statement BEST explains why historians use this term?
A It marked the first peaceful transfer of power between opposing political parties in American history
B It resulted in violent protests that nearly overthrew the government
C It was the first election where women were allowed to vote

Jefferson called it a "revolution" because power transferred peacefully from Federalists to Democratic-Republicans - proving the Constitution could handle political change without violence.
