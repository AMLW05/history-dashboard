Course Overview

Course Learning Outcomes (CLOs)

  1. CLO1: Analyze the social, political, and economic development of the United States from pre-Columbian times through 1865
  2. CLO2: Evaluate how diverse groups (Native Americans, Europeans, Africans, women) shaped American society and institutions
  3. CLO3: Apply historical thinking skills to interpret primary and secondary sources
  4. CLO4: Communicate historical arguments effectively through written and visual projects

Module 1

Indigenous America & First Contact

Prehistory-1675 ‚Ä¢ American Yawp Chapters 1 & 2

Inquiry Questions

  • Who lived in the Americas before 1492, and how did they organize their societies?
  • What happened when Europeans, Africans, and Indigenous peoples first encountered each other?
  • How did disease shape the outcomes of European colonization more than military conquest?

Module Learning Objectives (MLOs)

  1. Describe pre-Columbian Indigenous civilizations and their diverse societies (CLO1, CLO2)
  2. Analyze the effects of the Columbian Exchange on Indigenous and European populations (CLO1)
  3. Evaluate the role of Spanish colonialism in creating new racial hierarchies (CLO2, CLO3)

Module 2

Colonial Development

1620-1763 ‚Ä¢ American Yawp Chapters 3 & 4

Inquiry Questions

  • How did English colonists transform racial categories into fixed legal systems of slavery?
  • What role did religious and political conflicts play in shaping colonial development?
  • How did different groups resist or adapt to European colonization?

Module Learning Objectives (MLOs)

  1. Explain the development of race-based slavery in British North America (CLO1, CLO2)
  2. Analyze colonial rebellions and resistance movements (Bacon's Rebellion, Pueblo Revolt) (CLO2, CLO3)
  3. Evaluate the impact of the Great Awakening and consumer revolution on colonial identity (CLO1)
