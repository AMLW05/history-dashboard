Reform Movement Growth (1830-1860)

Analysis Questions

Q1: Which reform movement had the most members/circulation by 1855?
Answer: Temperance Movement with 1.7 million members
Q2: When did abolitionist societies experience most rapid growth?
Answer: 1835-1840 (grew from 200 to 1000 societies)
Q3: What event in 1848 corresponds with spike in women's rights petition signatures?
Answer: Seneca Falls Convention launched organized women's rights movement
Q4: Why did anti-slavery newspaper circulation grow steadily throughout period?
Answer: Growing Northern opposition to slavery expansion; improved printing technology; postal system growth
Q5: What does temperance movement's large membership reveal?
Answer: Moral reform attracted broader support than more radical causes; women's activism in 'acceptable' sphere
Q6: How might these growth trends have increased sectional tensions?
Answer: Abolition's growth alarmed South; Northern reform challenged Southern society; cultural divide widened
Q7: What role did women play across these movements?
Answer: Women dominated temperance, increasingly involved in abolition and women's rights; challenged separate spheres ideology
Q8: How did Second Great Awakening fuel this reform growth?
Answer: Religious revival emphasized moral responsibility to perfect society; created activist networks
