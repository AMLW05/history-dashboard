Q1 Multiple Choice (2.5 pts)
According to the data, which reform movement had the LARGEST membership/participation by 1855?
A Temperance Movement (1.7 million members)
B Abolitionist Societies (2,000 societies)
C Anti-Slavery Newspaper Circulation (300,000)
D Women's Rights Petition Signatures (200,000)
Temperance had 1.7 million members by 1855 - far exceeding other movements. This reflects temperance's broader social acceptability compared to more "radical" causes like abolition.
Q2 Ordering (2.5 pts)
Based on the data, place these reform movements in order from MOST to LEAST members/participants by 1855.
  1. Temperance Movement (1,700,000 members)
  2. Anti-Slavery Newspaper Circulation (300,000)
  3. Women's Rights Petition Signatures (200,000)
  4. Abolitionist Societies (2,000 societies - representing smaller total members)
Temperance's massive membership reflects its mainstream appeal. Note that 2,000 abolitionist societies represented perhaps 150,000-200,000 total members.
Q3 Numeric (2.5 pts)
According to the data, abolitionist societies grew from 50 in 1830 to 1,000 in 1840. By what percentage did the number of abolitionist societies increase during this decade?
Answer: 1900% [(1000-50)/50 x 100 = 1900%]
This explosive growth occurred during the 1830s when immediatism (demanding immediate abolition) replaced gradualism.
Q4 Multiple Answer (2.5 pts)
The data shows women's rights petition signatures spiked dramatically around 1848. Select ALL factors that explain this increase. (Select all that apply)
A The Seneca Falls Convention (1848) launched the organized women's rights movement
B Women gained experience organizing through abolition and temperance campaigns
C The Declaration of Sentiments demanded women's suffrage and equal rights
D The 19th Amendment was ratified in 1848 giving women the vote
The Seneca Falls Convention (1848) was a watershed moment. The 19th Amendment wasn't ratified until 1920.
Q5 Categorization (2.5 pts)
Categorize each factor as explaining why TEMPERANCE attracted more members OR why ABOLITION remained smaller despite rapid growth.
Why Temperance Had More Members
Seen as "respectable" reform acceptable for women's public participation
Did not challenge fundamental property rights or regional interests
Could be framed as protecting families from male drunkenness
Why Abolition Remained Smaller
Directly threatened Southern economic and social system
Faced violent opposition including mob attacks and gag rules
Divided even Northern communities over race and immediate vs. gradual approaches

Q7 Multiple Choice (2.5 pts)
The anti-slavery newspaper circulation data shows steady growth from 5,000 (1830) to 300,000 (1855). Which factor BEST explains this consistent upward trend?
A Improvements in printing technology, postal systems, and literacy rates expanded reach
B The federal government subsidized anti-slavery publications
C Southern states encouraged distribution of abolitionist materials
D Most newspapers in this era were dedicated to abolition
Technological changes (steam-powered printing, cheaper paper, expanded postal routes) combined with rising Northern literacy enabled mass distribution of papers like The Liberator.
Q8 Multiple Answer (2.5 pts)
Based on the growth patterns in the data, select ALL ways these reform trends likely INCREASED sectional tensions between North and South. (Select all that apply)
A Growing abolition movement threatened Southern slavery and way of life
B Northern reform culture contrasted with Southern resistance to social change
C Petition campaigns pressured Congress to address slavery, angering Southern representatives
D Southern states embraced temperance reform enthusiastically
Northern reform energy - especially abolition's explosive growth - directly challenged Southern institutions and widened the cultural divide that would lead to Civil War.

Q10 Multiple Choice (2.5 pts)
Analyzing all four data trends together, what do the patterns reveal about the relationship between reform movements in antebellum America?
A Reform movements were interconnected, with activists often participating in multiple causes and using similar organizing strategies
B Each reform movement operated completely independently with no overlap in membership or methods
C The government coordinated all reform movements through a central agency
D Reform movements declined steadily throughout the period
The parallel growth patterns suggest interconnection. Many reformers worked across movements, and tactics like petition campaigns were shared.
