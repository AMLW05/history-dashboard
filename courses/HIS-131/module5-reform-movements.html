Correct Answer Feedback
Correct! (1000-50)/50 x 100 = 1900% increase. This explosive growth occurred during the 1830s when immediatism (demanding immediate abolition) replaced gradualism.

Question 4
Canvas Type: Multiple Answer
2.5 points
Question Text
The data shows women's rights petition signatures spiked dramatically around 1848. Select ALL factors that explain this increase. (Select all that apply)
Answer Options (Mark multiple correct in Canvas)
A The Seneca Falls Convention (1848) launched the organized women's rights movement CORRECT
B Women gained experience organizing through abolition and temperance campaigns CORRECT
C The Declaration of Sentiments demanded women's suffrage and equal rights CORRECT
D The 19th Amendment was ratified in 1848 giving women the vote
Feedback
Correct Answer Feedback
Correct! The Seneca Falls Convention (1848) was a watershed moment. Women like Elizabeth Cady Stanton and Lucretia Mott applied organizing skills from other reform movements to demand their own rights.
Incorrect Answer Feedback
The 19th Amendment wasn't ratified until 1920. Focus on what happened in 1848 that catalyzed the women's rights movement.

Question 7
Canvas Type: Multiple Choice
2.5 points
Question Text
The anti-slavery newspaper circulation data shows steady growth from 5,000 (1830) to 300,000 (1855). Which factor BEST explains this consistent upward trend?
Answer Options (Enter all 4 in Canvas)
A Improvements in printing technology, postal systems, and literacy rates expanded reach CORRECT
B The federal government subsidized anti-slavery publications
C Southern states encouraged distribution of abolitionist materials
D Most newspapers in this era were dedicated to abolition
Feedback
Correct Answer Feedback
Correct! Technological changes (steam-powered printing, cheaper paper, expanded postal routes) combined with rising Northern literacy enabled mass distribution of papers like The Liberator and Frederick Douglass' North Star.
Incorrect Answer Feedback
The federal government actually restricted abolitionist mail in the South. Consider what technological and social changes enabled mass print media in this era.

Answer Options (Mark multiple correct in Canvas)
A Growing abolition movement threatened Southern slavery and way of life CORRECT
B Northern reform culture contrasted with Southern resistance to social change CORRECT
C Petition campaigns pressured Congress to address slavery, angering Southern representatives CORRECT

Feedback
Correct Answer Feedback
Correct! Northern reform energy - especially abolition's explosive growth - directly challenged Southern institutions and widened the cultural divide that would lead to Civil War.

Question 10
Canvas Type: Multiple Choice
2.5 points
Question Text
Analyzing all four data trends together, what do the patterns reveal about the relationship between reform movements in antebellum America?
Answer Options (Enter all 4 in Canvas)
A Reform movements were interconnected, with activists often participating in multiple causes and using similar organizing strategies CORRECT
B Each reform movement operated completely independently with no overlap in membership or methods
C The government coordinated all reform movements through a central agency
D Reform movements declined steadily throughout the period
Feedback
Correct Answer Feedback
Correct! The data shows parallel growth patterns suggesting interconnection. Many reformers (like the Grimke sisters) worked across movements, and tactics like petition campaigns were shared. This "reform network" created a culture of activism.
Incorrect Answer Feedback
Notice how all four movements show growth during the same period. What might explain these parallel patterns? Consider whether reformers might have participated in multiple movements.
